Being a Woman in Tech

Just in case you haven’t heard, it can be hard being a woman in IT. Being taken professionally can be difficult and emotionally taxing when you’re forced to be constantly aware of gender and its consequences in the office. But here I don’t want to lament on the problems women know and experience daily. A never-ending avalanche of those posts make the news rounds hourly. And that’s the problem. The issue continues and there’s no action taken.

It’s a high time for a change. And it starts now.

Why we have too few women leaders

Success and likeability are positively correlated for men and negatively correlated for women, encapsulating and confirming the truth that women in the workplace have long-known to be true. Being likeable and successful is difficult, if not entirely impossible in workplace environments where the glass ceiling is so low women can only crawl towards equality.

Debbie Sterling

Debbie Sterling (born February 26, 1983) is an American engineer, businesswoman and the founder and CEO of GoldieBlox. Sterling is an engineer, spokesperson, and advocate for women in engineering and technology. Sterling was named Time's Person of the Moment and Business Insider's 30 Women Who Are Changing the World.
